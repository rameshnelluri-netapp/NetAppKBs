Section 01

Why Disaster Recovery Matters

Enterprise storage must guarantee two things during a failure: no data loss (RPO = 0) and no downtime (RTO ≈ 0). NetApp provides multiple technologies to achieve this, and understanding which layer each one protects is the key to designing a bulletproof storage architecture.

The Recovery Spectrum
Core Concept RPO (Recovery Point Objective) = how much data can you afford to lose?
RTO (Recovery Time Objective) = how long can you afford to be down?
Both must be zero for Tier-0 mission-critical workloads.
Section 02

What is NetApp MetroCluster?

MetroCluster is not just replication — it is a stretched hardware cluster that mirrors the entire storage infrastructure across two geographically separate sites. When one site fails, the surviving site takes over automatically and transparently, typically within 60 seconds, with zero data loss.

Think of it as RAID-1 for your entire data centre — not just your disks, but your controllers, aggregates, LUNs, NFS paths, and network identity.

🔄

Automatic Failover

No human intervention required. Hosts don't see a disruption — paths fail over via ALUA/multipathing.

🏗️

Infrastructure Mirror

Mirrors controllers, aggregates, LUNs, SVMs, and config — not just volume data.

⚡

Zero RPO

Every write is committed to both sites synchronously before the application gets an ACK.

🌐

Metro Distance

Supported up to 300km between sites (IP MetroCluster). FC MetroCluster supports up to ~700km.

🔒

FC & NAS Support

Works with FC SAN, iSCSI, NFS, CIFS/SMB. WWPNs and LUN IDs remain the same after failover.

🛡️

Split-Brain Protection

Mediator (3rd site) or Tiebreaker software prevents both sites from claiming ownership simultaneously.

Section 03

MetroCluster Architecture

A MetroCluster configuration consists of two HA pairs (one per site) connected via inter-switch links (ISLs). Each site has its own controllers, switches, and disk shelves, but they operate as a single ONTAP cluster.

MetroCluster Two-Site Architecture
Key Insight — Two Plexes per Aggregate Every aggregate in a MetroCluster has exactly two plexes: one on local disk shelves and one on remote disk shelves. SyncMirror keeps both plexes in sync. This is the foundation of zero-RPO.

Section 04

SyncMirror — The Engine Inside MetroCluster

SyncMirror is the RAID-level synchronous mirroring technology that powers MetroCluster. It operates below the ONTAP file system and ensures every write is committed to both plexes before acknowledging to the host.

SyncMirror Write Path
Why this guarantees RPO = 0 The host never receives an ACK until both plexes have confirmed the write. If Site A explodes 1 millisecond after the ACK, the data is already safely on Site B's disks. There is no window for data loss.

MetroCluster Failover Process (Automatic)

  • 1Site A loses power / connectivity. Nodes on Site A stop responding.
  • 2Mediator/Tiebreaker detects the failure and grants Site B the right to take over (prevents split-brain).
  • 3Site B performs Automatic Unplanned Switchover (AUFO) — takes ownership of all aggregates.
  • 4The remote plexes on Site B are promoted to active. Data is fully accessible.
  • 5SAN hosts reconnect automatically via surviving FC/iSCSI paths — same WWPNs, same LUN IDs.
  • 6NAS clients experience a brief mount pause, then reconnect — no remapping needed.
Section 05

SnapMirror Synchronous (SM-S)

SnapMirror Sync is a volume-level replication technology that synchronously replicates data from a source volume to a destination volume on another cluster, ensuring RPO = 0 for individual volumes or groups of volumes.

Unlike MetroCluster, SM-S protects data — not the entire storage infrastructure. After a failure, someone (or something via ONTAP Mediator) must actively trigger a failover process.

SnapMirror Sync Write Path
What happens after failure with SM-S? After Site A goes down: (1) Mirror relationship enters "broken-off" state. (2) Admin must manually run snapmirror break. (3) Destination volume becomes read-write. (4) Applications must be pointed to the new volume path. This takes minutes to hours, not seconds.

SM-S Policies

Policy TypeRPODescription

StrictSyncZeroSame as Sync but I/O is halted if mirror is broken — no divergence allowed.

SnapMirror Business Continuity (SMBC / AutomatedFailover) SMBC is SM-S with automatic failover triggered by ONTAP Mediator. It delivers near-MetroCluster RTO (~60–120s) for SAN (iSCSI, FC) workloads without requiring full MetroCluster hardware. But it still operates at the volume level — not the full infrastructure level.
Section 06

SVM Disaster Recovery (SVM-DR)

SVM-DR replicates an entire Storage Virtual Machine (SVM) — including all volumes, LUNs, NFS/CIFS export policies, LIFS, igroups, and schedules — to a destination cluster. It is a comprehensive DR for all workloads inside an SVM, but with async replication (minutes of RPO) and manual failover (hours of RTO).

SVM-DR Replication Scope
SVM-DR Limitation After a site failure, SVM-DR requires: (1) breaking the SnapMirror relationship, (2) activating the DR SVM, (3) bringing up LIFs with potentially new IPs, (4) remounting NFS exports, (5) rescanning LUNs. This process typically takes 30 minutes to several hours depending on automation.

Section 08

Workload Tiering & Technology Selection

The right protection technology depends entirely on the business impact of downtime and data loss for each workload. Here is the standard tiering model:

TierWorkloadsRPORTORecommended Technology
Tier-0 Mission Critical Oracle RAC, SQL Always-On, Core Banking, Trading Systems, VMware vSphere HA clusters Zero < 60s MetroCluster
Tier-1 Business Critical ERP, CRM, Tier-1 databases, specific critical volumes within a cluster Zero 1–10 min SnapMirror Sync / SMBC
Tier-2 Business Important Dev/test, web servers, file sharing, email, DR copies of Tier-1 Minutes 1–4 hrs SVM-DR + Async SnapMirror
Tier-3 Standard Archives, backups, non-critical workloads Hours Hours–Days SnapVault / Object Store
Best Practice: Layered Architecture MetroCluster and SnapMirror technologies are complementary, not competing. A well-designed enterprise architecture typically uses:
  • MetroCluster for Tier-0 within metro distance (<300km)
  • SnapMirror Sync / SMBC for Tier-1 volumes needing zero RPO without full MC hardware
  • SVM-DR + async SnapMirror for long-distance DR (>300km) and Tier-2 workloads
  • SnapVault for backup retention and compliance
Layered DR Architecture — Reference Design
The Key Answer to "Why MetroCluster if we have SnapMirror Sync?" SnapMirror Sync guarantees your data survives. MetroCluster guarantees your business survives — no downtime, no manual steps, no re-mapping, no re-zoning, no host-side intervention. For Tier-0 workloads where every second of downtime costs thousands of dollars, MetroCluster's automatic, transparent, infrastructure-level failover is irreplaceable.
